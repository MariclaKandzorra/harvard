    • BUSINESS MODEL OF WALT DISEY
      • BUSINESS MODEL DESIGN

        Walt Disney created a whole new product chain which can be named as an offline platform that sells b2b and b2c, while customers today can fractionless switch to vendors of Walt Disney branded products

        We all know from Designing for viral growth in the world of digital platform marketing that pull strategies rather than push strategies are most important and effective. Pushing goods, services and the messages about them between customers is no longer the key of success since in the networked world access to marketing and communication channels is democratized, people are more distracted inside an array of endless competing options. Creating awareness alone doesn't drive adoption and usage. Instead those products - goods and services - must be designed to be so attractive that they naturally pull customers into their orbit.

        Back in 1957 Walt Disney was already very aware of the need of a baked into marketing known today as positive feedback loops that improve and extend the offering, customer base and revenue between Disney's Films, Music, TV, Magazines, Books, Disneyland and Merchandising.

        Additionally designed Walt Disney its business model highly scalable by turning every story and character into brands which then turn the entire product wheel from Disney's Films, Music, TV, Magazines, Books, Disneyland and Merchandising.

        Walt Disney looked in its business model design across strategic groups (these are clusters of companies with similar strategy), across the chain of buyers (these are obvious customer groups), across complementary products and services (these are combined values in each industry) in order to create new market space, it identified the factors that determine buyer's decisions to trade up from one group to another, it targeted all customers in this groups involved in the buying decision (children and adults), and seek untapped value hidden in other industries' offerings that affect Disney's offerings' value it creates with its branded stories and characters.

        That's what we call today an holistic product chain, which makes dream come true or reality by transforming stories and sounds into pictures and movies, for then transforming them into toys and other products, for then meeting them on tv and radio, for then playing with them in games virtual and for real in costumes, for then transforming them into reality by creating worlds and parks in their holiday resorts and theme parks and on the Disney cruise line. So, that Disney realized already in 1957 the jump from commercial consumption of products and services into the world of adventure and customer experiences all based on their unique brands and stories.

    • SWOT ANALYSIS OF WALT DISNEY

      OPPORTUNITIES

      1. DIVERSIFICATION THROUGH ACQUISITION - Disney started early to diversify in order to reach children and adults alike, eg. in late 2019, Disney acquired 21st Century Fox and gained rights of 21st Century Fox's vast library content. It acquired also its streaming service Hulu and National Geographics. The main reason of acquisition of Walt Disney corporate strategy is the collection of content and talent. Combination of both Disney and Fox will create dynamic and transformative era as media industry is changing progressively. Required is therefore demand and innovation in the competitive environment for stability and growth of the organization.
      1. MOVIE RIGHTS - currently Disney is not focused on cable channels like ESPN bc of steady growth rate but is now focused on online video streaming business that is direct to customer business like Netflix. Disney reduced further its competitors, such as Pixar and 21st Century Fox, by acquiring them. Disney's recent M&A transactions especially of 21st Century Fox has made Disney more powerful in the media business that assists in to stay in the top position. It has further developed a new platform of Disney+ which portrary its veriaties of contents which is received through Disney's diversification strategy. Due to isolation caused by COVID-19 worldwide media consumption was boosted, which Disney+ would profit from in the next quarters. So, that it is expected that Disney's M&A plans in the media segment to be successful in the long run.

      THREATS

      1. INCREASED REGULATORY - many new laws and regulations regarding privacy, data protection, safety, licensing and distribution have created consumer trend changes which have the potential to reduce company profits and restrict expansions into new markets. Other kind of actions include renewals of licenses with the risk of denial of license renewal or revocation of it. The new laws affect also that the chances of being fined in case of violations are higher. The fines are also larger now. Regarding ownership of television and radio channels the laws impose limits on the numbers of stations, so that Disney does not have more than one station in every market.
      1. ECONOMIC FLUCTIATIONS (eg due to Covid): In the past too, economic declines have had a negative impact on the demand for and spending at its parks and resorts as well as its earnings from advertisements and other sources. The decline is especially reality during lock downs, besides the .
      1. TECHNOLOGY ADVANCEMENT: Technological change are happening fast. Disney's profitability depends on its ability to adopt new technologies and adapt to the shift patterns of consumer demands.
      1. COMPETITIVE PRESSURE: Disney is facing strong competition in all the areas of its operations including tourism and entertainment. Higher competitive pressure can increase further due to technology and changes in market dynamics limiting chances of growth.
      1. SEASONALITY: Advertising revenues of Disney are generally higher during the fall and lower during summer. Parks and resorts see higher occupancy and attendance during the vacations in summer months or early-winter and spring-holiday periods. Studio entertainment is also affected by the timing of the releases. Also in Consumer Products & Interactive Media segments consumer purchasing behaviour varies by season.
